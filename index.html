History of Valentine's Day

Valentine's Day, also called Saint Valentine's Day or the Feast of Saint Valentine, is celebrated annually on February 14. It originated as a Christian feast day honoring a martyr named Valentine, and through later folk traditions it has also become a significant cultural, religious and commercial celebration of romance and love in many regions of the world.

Here are some traditions

  • People send greeting cards and gifts
  • People go on dates.
  • People attend church services.
  • People participate in novenas.
  • People recreate favorite dates.
  • People cook together

The holiday's origins are uncertain, but some believe it may have originated from the Roman festival of Lupercalia. Others believe it commemorates the death of a saint named Valentine. Historians have theories that several saints named Valentine may have inspired the holiday. .
